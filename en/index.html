[Paper Notes] Highly imbalanced fault diagnosis of mechanical systems based on wavelet packet distortion and convolutional neural networks

Mechanical Fault Diagnosis via WPD-CNN

Journal: Advanced Engineering Informatics, Vol. 51, 2022, 101535

DOI: 10.1016/j.aei.2022.101535

1. Research Background

In the field of mechanical system monitoring, automatic fault diagnosis technology is of great significance for ensuring industrial safety. However, the core challenge in actual engineering scenarios is Class Imbalance: mechanical equipment operates in a normal state for a long time, resulting in abundant health samples but extremely scarce fault samples.

When traditional deep learning models are trained on highly imbalanced datasets (e.g., health to fault ratio of 80:1), they tend to show obvious classification bias, predicting all samples as the majority class (health), leading to missed faults. Existing data augmentation methods (like random oversampling or SMOTE) tend to cause overfitting or introduce irrelevant noise when handling very few fault samples.

2. Technical Principle: Wavelet Packet Distortion (WPD)

This paper proposes a data augmentation strategy based on Wavelet Packet Distortion (WPD), generating diverse new samples by performing physically meaningful transformations on the original signals in the time-frequency domain.

Fig 1. Flowchart of Wavelet Packet Distortion

2.1 Signal Decomposition and Transformation

The method first uses Wavelet Packet Transform (WPT) to decompose the original vibration signal into wavelet packet coefficients in different frequency bands. Compared to standard wavelet transform, WPT provides finer frequency resolution to capture fault features completely.

2.2 Nonlinear Distortion Processing

Specific nodes in the decomposed coefficient space are randomly selected for nonlinear processing. The processing function is defined as:

$$ \tilde{w} = \text{sign}(w) \cdot (\text{abs}(w))^d $$

Where w is the original coefficient, and d is the distortion coefficient randomly selected within a preset range (e.g., [0.8, 1.2]). The physical logic is that fault features are usually associated with specific frequency components. By adjusting d, the specific shape of the waveform is altered while retaining the original frequency distribution characteristics.

Fig 2. Sine signal and its distorted versions

2.3 Signal Reconstruction

The modified coefficients are reconstructed into a time-series signal using Inverse WPT. The generated samples are mixed with original samples to build a class-balanced training set.

3. Classification Model Architecture: ConvNet

The paper utilizes a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) as the end-to-end classifier. Since the dataset is balanced after WPD augmentation, CNN can effectively learn deep features to distinguish different health states. The model includes Convolutional layers, Batch Normalization (BN), ReLU activation, and Global Average Pooling (GAP), outputting results via Softmax.

4. Experimental Verification

4.1 Experimental Setup

Experimental data comes from a ground simulation test bench for civil aviation hydraulic pumps. The dataset contains 1 health state and 5 fault states. To simulate extreme imbalance, only 5 samples are selected for each fault class for training, while the health class contains 400 training samples.

5. Conclusion

Wavelet Packet Distortion, as a signal-level data augmentation method, can effectively alleviate the small sample and imbalance problems in mechanical fault diagnosis. It is computationally efficient, does not rely on complex generative networks, and retains original physical features, making it suitable for predictive maintenance in key industrial fields.
